### GLP-1 Drug Impact on CPG Demand

- **April 8 — Morgan Stanley Report**: Updated GLP-1 analysis — estimates 24M Americans now on GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound). By 2028, projected 40M users.
- **Measured Impact on CPG Categories** (NielsenIQ data, areas with highest GLP-1 adoption):
  - Sugary carbonated beverages: -8% volume
  - Salty snacks: -6% volume
  - Ice cream/frozen desserts: -7% volume
  - Candy/chocolate: -5% volume
  - Alcohol (beer/wine/spirits): -4% volume
  - **Growing categories among GLP-1 users**: Protein bars (+22%), protein shakes (+18%), Greek yogurt (+12%), sugar-free beverages (+15%)
- **PepsiCo Response**: Frito-Lay launching "Protein Pops" line (protein-enriched chips). Pepsi launching "Pepsi Protein" sparkling water with 10g protein.
- **Nestlé Response**: New "Vital Pursuit" frozen meal line specifically designed for GLP-1 users — high protein, low calorie, portion-controlled. Launching in Walmart and Target in May 2026.
- **Coca-Cola Response**: Accelerating zero-sugar portfolio. Launched "Coke Protein+" (zero sugar + 5g protein) in test markets.

### Net Demand Assessment

**Overall CPG demand signal: MIXED — Category-dependent divergence**

The consumer goods market is experiencing a clear bifurcation:
- **Winners**: Protein-enriched products, zero-sugar beverages, private-label/value products, summer seasonal categories, functional foods
- **Losers**: Traditional sugary snacks/beverages (GLP-1 effect), premium branded products losing to private label, chocolate/coffee (cost-driven price increases causing demand destruction)

Key catalyst to watch: Summer heat intensity (May-June) for seasonal demand uplift, and GLP-1 user growth trajectory for structural demand reshaping. The next 4-8 weeks will be defined by the tension between weather-driven demand tailwinds and inflation/GLP-1-driven demand headwinds.
