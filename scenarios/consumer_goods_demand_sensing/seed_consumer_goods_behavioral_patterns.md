# Consumer Goods Demand Sensing — Behavioral Patterns & Participant Analysis

## 1. How Different Market Participants React to Demand Signals

### 1.1 CPG Brand Executives — The Strategic Responders

**Behavioral Pattern**: CPG brand executives (CMOs, VP of Sales, Category Managers) operate on quarterly planning cycles but must react to demand signals in real-time. Their responses follow a hierarchy:
1. **Price increases**: First response to cost pressures — pass through commodity inflation via pack-price architecture (shrinkflation, list price increases, trade spend reduction)
2. **Innovation pivots**: Launch products aligned with trending demands (protein, zero-sugar, clean-label) — 6-12 month cycle from concept to shelf
3. **Portfolio pruning**: Cut underperforming SKUs to focus on high-velocity items — typically announced during earnings calls
4. **Marketing reallocation**: Shift advertising spend toward growing categories/channels — can happen within weeks

**Communication Style**: Quarterly earnings calls (most impactful), investor day presentations, press releases, industry conferences (CAGNY, Consumer Analyst Group of New York). CEOs and CMOs increasingly active on LinkedIn. Trade press interviews in publications like Ad Age, Marketing Week, and Food Business News.

**Social Media Presence**: Corporate accounts on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Instagram primarily for brand marketing, not strategic communication. However, executive LinkedIn posts about consumer trends increasingly move industry narrative. Company press releases picked up by Bloomberg, Reuters, CNBC.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When multiple Tier-1 CPG companies report negative volume growth in the same category during the same quarter, it signals structural demand shift, not just one company's execution issue.

### 1.2 Retail Buyers & Category Managers — The Demand Gatekeepers

**Behavioral Pattern**: Retail buyers at Walmart, Kroger, Target, and Costco are the most important demand sensing nodes in the CPG ecosystem. They see real-time POS data, control shelf space allocation, and make assortment decisions that directly create or destroy demand:
1. **Shelf space reallocation**: Moving facings from declining brands to growing ones — signals a demand shift 4-8 weeks before it shows in market share data
2. **Private-label expansion**: When retailers expand store-brand assortment in a category, it signals both consumer demand for value and retailer confidence in the category's importance
3. **Promotional strategy**: Shifting promotional dollars from national brands to private label, or increasing promotional frequency on staples = consumers need price relief
4. **New vendor onboarding**: When major retailers rapidly onboard DTC/emerging brands (e.g., Celsius, Olipop), it signals demand validation

**Communication Style**: Retail buyers communicate primarily through purchase orders, planogram resets, and vendor scorecards — not public channels. However, retailer earnings calls, investor days, and executive media appearances reveal buyer strategy. Walmart's "Open Call" for new suppliers and Target's partnership announcements signal category priorities.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: Walmart's inventory-to-sales ratio changes in specific categories are a leading indicator. Rising I/S ratio = demand slowing faster than expected (potential markdowns coming). Falling I/S ratio = demand stronger than forecasted (potential out-of-stocks).

### 1.3 Supply Chain & Demand Planning Managers — The Quantitative Forecasters

**Behavioral Pattern**: Demand planners at CPG and retail companies use statistical models, machine learning, and judgment to forecast demand 4-52 weeks ahead. They are the first to see emerging demand signals and translate them into action:
1. **Forecast adjustments**: Weekly bias checks — when actuals consistently exceed forecasts, demand planners increase forecast, triggering production and procurement changes
2. **Safety stock changes**: Increasing safety stock levels = expecting higher demand variability. Decreasing = expecting stable/declining demand
3. **Promotional uplift modeling**: Pre-event demand forecasts for promotions, holidays, weather events
4. **Exception management**: Flagging abnormal demand patterns (sudden spikes or drops) to commercial teams

**Communication Style**: Internal S&OP (Sales & Operations Planning) meetings, demand review sessions, and exception reports. Externally, demand planners share insights at supply chain conferences (Gartner Supply Chain Symposium, IBF's Business Planning & Forecasting Conference), LinkedIn thought leadership, and trade publications (Journal of Business Forecasting, Supply Chain Dive).

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When multiple CPG demand planners simultaneously increase short-term forecasts for a category (visible through supplier order patterns), it confirms a demand signal is real, not noise.

### 1.4 Market Research Analysts — The Data Interpreters

**Behavioral Pattern**: Analysts at NielsenIQ, Circana, Kantar, and consulting firms (McKinsey, Bain, BCG) are the narrators of CPG demand. They transform raw data into stories that influence industry decisions:
1. **Weekly/monthly POS reports**: Category-level sales trends delivered to CPG and retail clients — shapes how the industry perceives demand
2. **Trend reports**: "State of the Industry" publications that label emerging trends (e.g., "The GLP-1 Effect on CPG") — these reports create industry consensus
3. **Media appearances**: Analysts on CNBC, Bloomberg, and industry podcasts translating data into narratives
4. **Conference presentations**: NielsenIQ Connects, Circana Summits — where new data and trend labels are launched

**Communication Style**: Published research reports (paywall), press releases with headline data points (free), media interviews, LinkedIn posts, conference keynotes. NielsenIQ and Circana data releases are treated as "earnings reports" for the CPG industry.

**Social Media Influence**: Industry analysts with large LinkedIn followings (50K+) can set the narrative for weeks. A NielsenIQ LinkedIn post titled "Private Label Hits Record 25% Share" gets thousands of shares and shapes retailer and CPG strategy conversations.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When NielsenIQ and Circana data align on a trend (e.g., "protein-enriched products growing 3x category average"), it becomes industry consensus within 2-3 weeks, triggering investment and innovation decisions.

### 1.5 Consumers — The Ultimate Demand Signal

**Behavioral Pattern**: Consumer behavior varies significantly by segment:

**Price-Sensitive Consumers (HH income <$50K, ~35% of US households)**
- First to switch to private label during inflation
- Most responsive to promotions, coupons, and BOGO deals
- Dollar store and discount grocer shoppers
- Reduce purchase frequency before switching brands entirely
- Social media: Budget tip communities, coupon groups, r/Frugal

**Middle-Market Consumers (HH income $50K-$100K, ~30% of US households)**
- "Selective trading down" — keep premium brands in favorite categories, switch to private label in commoditized categories
- Responsive to value messaging ("same quality, lower price")
- Club store (Costco/Sam's) shoppers seeking bulk value
- Social media: Costco haul videos, "dupe" content, family meal planning

**Premium Consumers (HH income >$100K, ~35% of US households)**
- Less price-sensitive but increasingly "value-conscious" (willing to pay more for perceived quality, not brand names alone)
- Early adopters of trends (plant-based, organic, functional foods, GLP-1 friendly)
- Whole Foods, specialty grocery, and premium e-commerce shoppers
- Social media: Wellness influencers, clean eating, "what I eat in a day" content

**Gen Z Consumers (18-29, across income levels)**
- Discover products primarily through TikTok and Instagram
- Brand loyalty is low — variety-seeking and trend-driven
- Over-index on snacking, energy drinks, and functional beverages
- Willing to pay for sustainability, social impact, and "cool" brands
- Social media: TikTok product reviews, Instagram Reels, BeReal

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When consumer behavior changes are visible across multiple segments simultaneously (e.g., both price-sensitive and premium consumers increasing protein purchases), it signals a structural demand shift, not just a segment-specific reaction.

### 1.6 Social Media Influencers & Content Creators — The Demand Amplifiers

**Behavioral Pattern**: Food, health, and lifestyle influencers are now the most powerful demand creation force in CPG. Their influence operates through:
1. **Product discovery**: First exposure to new products often comes through influencer content, not advertising
2. **Viral demand spikes**: A single TikTok can drive 300-500% demand increases within 48 hours. The supply chain rarely keeps up, causing stockouts that further amplify viral demand.
3. **Trend creation**: Influencers don't just react to trends — they create them. #ProteinEverything, #75Hard, #WhatIEatInADay all originated from creator content.
4. **Brand destruction**: Negative content ("ingredients I'll never eat," "brands that lie to you") can cause lasting demand damage

**Communication Style**: Short-form video (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts) for reach. Long-form YouTube for depth. Instagram Stories for daily recommendations. Podcasts for wellness and nutrition deep dives.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When a product or category appears in 3+ unrelated influencer videos within a week (without being paid sponsorships), organic viral demand is building. Track TikTok hashtag velocity — exponential view growth signals a demand spike 3-7 days before it hits retail POS data.

### 1.7 Commodity Traders & Agricultural Analysts — The Cost Signal Providers

**Behavioral Pattern**: Commodity traders and agricultural analysts influence CPG demand indirectly through input costs. When cocoa, coffee, sugar, wheat, or packaging materials spike in price, CPG companies must decide whether to absorb costs (margin hit) or pass through to consumers (demand risk).

**Communication Style**: Market reports from USDA, ICE Futures, CME Group. Analyst notes from Rabobank (agricultural specialty), StoneX, Olam. Bloomberg Commodity Index coverage. Agricultural trade press (Successful Farming, DTN Progressive Farmer).

**Social Media Influence**: Commodity price charts shared on FinTwit and LinkedIn spark CPG industry concern. "Cocoa hits $10,000/tonne" headlines drive chocolate price increase discussions across media.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When 2+ key CPG input commodities rise simultaneously above 12-month averages, a wave of consumer price increases follows in 2-4 months, triggering the private-label switching cycle.

### 1.8 FDA & Regulatory Officials — The Rule Changers

**Behavioral Pattern**: Regulatory actions create step-function demand changes:
1. **Food recalls**: Immediate demand destruction for affected brand, demand surge for competitors. Can take 6-12 months for recalled brand to recover.
2. **Labeling changes**: New front-of-pack nutrition labels (e.g., Chile-style warning labels) shift demand from high-sugar/sodium products to "healthier" alternatives over 12-24 months.
3. **Ingredient bans**: FDA banning specific ingredients (Red Dye No. 3, titanium dioxide) forces reformulation and can create temporary stockouts.
4. **Health claims approval**: FDA approving health claims for specific ingredients (e.g., fiber → heart health) creates category growth.

**Communication Style**: Federal Register notices, FDA press releases, congressional hearings, industry comment periods. Media coverage amplifies regulatory actions.

**Demand Sensing Signal**: When FDA announces a proposed rule change (not yet final), CPG companies begin reformulating and retailers begin adjusting assortments — creating demand shifts 6-12 months before the rule takes effect.

## 2. Demand Sensing Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The Celsius Energy Drink Explosion (2022-2025)
- **Signal Origin**: Fitness influencers on TikTok organically featuring Celsius in workout content
- **Demand Chain**: Influencer content → trial purchasing by Gen Z/Millennial gym-goers → POS velocity increase at convenience stores → Costco onboarding → massive distribution expansion → mainstream adoption
- **Timeline**: 18 months from TikTok virality to #3 US energy drink brand
- **Demand Sensing Lesson**: Social media velocity preceded POS data by 4-6 weeks. Retailers who tracked TikTok mentions gained shelf space advantage.

### Case Study 2: Egg Price Crisis (2022-2023)
- **Signal Origin**: Avian flu outbreak reducing egg supply
- **Demand Chain**: Supply shortage → price spike ($7+/dozen) → consumer outrage on social media → demand destruction (consumers buying less) → substitution (egg alternatives, fewer baking occasions) → private label egg share surge → eventual price normalization → demand recovery but not to pre-crisis levels
- **Timeline**: 6-month acute phase, 12-month full cycle
- **Demand Sensing Lesson**: Social media sentiment (outrage velocity) predicted demand destruction 2-3 weeks before it showed in POS volume data.

### Case Study 3: GLP-1 Impact on Snack Demand (2024-Present)
- **Signal Origin**: Ozempic/Wegovy adoption reaching critical mass
- **Demand Chain**: GLP-1 users reducing calorie intake → snack/soda volume declining in high-adoption zip codes → NielsenIQ/Circana publishing correlation data → media amplification ("Ozempic is killing the snack industry") → CPG companies launching GLP-1-friendly products → category bifurcation (traditional snacks declining, protein snacks growing)
- **Timeline**: Ongoing, accelerating
- **Demand Sensing Lesson**: Pharmacy prescription data (GLP-1 fills by geography) is a leading indicator of CPG demand shifts by market. CPG companies tracking GLP-1 adoption by DMA gain 3-6 month planning advantage.

### Case Study 4: Trader Joe's & Aldi Social Media Effect (2023-2026)
- **Signal Origin**: TikTok "haul" and "dupe" culture making discount grocers aspirational
- **Demand Chain**: Viral content showing Aldi/TJ's products → perception shift from "cheap" to "smart shopping" → foot traffic increase among Millennials and Gen Z → traditional grocers losing share → private label normalization across retail
- **Timeline**: Ongoing structural shift
- **Demand Sensing Lesson**: Social media sentiment toward private-label brands shifted from negative/neutral to positive 6-9 months before market share data confirmed the trend. TikTok view velocity on #AldiFinds was predictive of same-store sales growth.

## 3. Cross-Participant Demand Signal Propagation Map

```
[Commodity Price Spike] → [CPG Cost Increase] → [Retail Price Increase] → [Consumer Pushback]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Private Label Switch]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Social Media Discussion]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Media Amplification]
                                                                              ↓
                                                              [More Consumers Aware → More Switching]

[TikTok Viral Content] → [Consumer Trial Purchase] → [POS Velocity Spike] → [Retailer Reorder]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Shelf Space Expansion]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [More Consumer Access → More Sales]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [NielsenIQ/Circana Reports "Trend"]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Industry Investment → Category Growth]

[Weather Forecast] → [Demand Planner Forecast Adjust] → [Production Increase] → [Retail Stocking]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Consumer Availability → Purchase]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [If Stockout → Social Media Complaints]
                                                                              ↓
                                                                    [Competitor Substitution]
```

These propagation patterns typically follow 3-7 day cycles for social media-driven demand, 2-4 week cycles for macro/economic-driven demand, and 3-6 month cycles for structural shifts (GLP-1, regulatory changes).
